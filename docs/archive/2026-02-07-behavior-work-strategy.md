## 2. Industry Context & Problem

### 2.1 The ABA Market

Applied Behavior Analysis therapy is a high-growth, highly fragmented market. Thousands of small and mid-sized agencies provide in-home, center-based, school-based, and telehealth ABA services to children and families. The market is defined by a few structural realities that shape every agency's day-to-day:

- **Client acquisition is the #1 revenue driver.** A single new client represents $40,000–$80,000+ in annual revenue. Agencies live and die by their ability to fill their caseload.
- **The intake-to-active pipeline is long and complex.** A family's journey from first contact to receiving services involves insurance verification, authorization requests, assessments, report writing, and approval — often spanning weeks or months. Families drop off at every stage.
- **Communication gaps lose families.** When agencies don't proactively update families on their status, families assume they've been forgotten and seek services elsewhere. The agencies that communicate well win.
- **Operations tooling is fragmented.** Agencies cobble together Google Forms, spreadsheets, generic CRMs, email, and paper processes to manage intake and client information. Clinical practice management tools (CentralReach, Catalyst) handle session data and billing but don't address the business operations layer.
- **Staff hiring is a secondary but persistent constraint.** RBT turnover is high (40–60% annually). Agencies always need staff, but they only hire when they have or anticipate clients. Client growth drives hiring, not the reverse.

### 2.2 The Gap We Fill

There is no dominant, ABA-specific platform that addresses the business operations layer — the space between a family's first contact and the clinical practice management system. That gap includes:

- How families discover the agency (directory, website, referral)
- How inquiries and intakes are captured and organized
- How families are communicated with throughout the intake process
- How insurance, authorization, and assessment workflows are tracked
- How client information is stored and accessed across the agency
- How tasks and follow-ups are managed per client

Behavior Work fills this entire gap with one integrated platform. It is not a clinical tool and does not compete with practice management software. It is the pre-clinical and business operations layer that every agency needs and very few have.

## 4. The Client Lifecycle Model

The core concept of the product is the client lifecycle — the full journey of a family from first contact through discharge. Every feature in the platform either feeds into, supports, or operates within this lifecycle.

### 4.1 Lifecycle Stages

The following stages represent the journey every ABA client goes through. These stages already exist in our client management system and form the backbone of the product:

| Stage | What's Happening | Agency Needs at This Stage |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Inquiry** | Family submits a contact form or intake form. They've expressed interest but haven't provided full details. | Respond quickly. Acknowledge receipt. Collect missing information. Build trust. |
| **Intake Pending** | Agency has the family's information and is collecting remaining details, verifying insurance, preparing documentation. | Request additional docs. Verify insurance eligibility. Keep family informed. |
| **Waitlist** | Agency cannot serve the family immediately (full caseload, scheduling constraints). Family is waiting for an opening. | Keep family engaged. Share resources. Provide periodic updates. Prevent dropout. |
| **Assessment** | Insurance has authorized (or agency is requesting authorization for) the initial assessment. Assessment may be scheduled or completed. | Request authorization. Schedule assessment. Confirm appointment. Prepare report. Submit to insurance. |
| **Active** | Family is receiving ABA services. Ongoing management of authorizations, reassessments, and team coordination. | Track authorizations. Monitor reauthorization dates. Manage team assignments. Share resources. |
| **On Hold** | Services are temporarily paused (family request, insurance issue, scheduling gap). | Track reason. Set follow-up reminders. Maintain relationship. |
| **Discharged** | Services have ended. Client record is retained for reference. | Document discharge. Retain records. Enable reactivation if needed. |

### 4.2 How Features Map to the Lifecycle

| Feature | Role in Lifecycle | Stage(s) Served |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Contact Form | First point of capture. Family expresses interest. | Entry → Inquiry |
| Intake Form | Detailed information collection. Captures insurance, child details, parent info. | Entry → Inquiry / Intake Pending |
| FindABATherapy Listing | Discovery channel. Families find the agency through directory search. | Entry → Inquiry |
| Inquiry Inbox | Where new submissions land. Triage and respond. | Inquiry |
| Client Profiles | Full record of family information, insurance, authorizations, documents. | All stages |
| Communication Templates | Branded emails sent to families at each lifecycle stage. | All stages |
| Task Management | Track what needs to be done for each client. | All stages |
| Insurance Tracking | Track policies, eligibility, member IDs. | Intake Pending → Active |
| Authorization Tracking | Track service authorizations, approvals, denials, hours. | Assessment → Active |
| Document Management | Store insurance cards, assessments, IEPs, consents. | All stages |
| Parent Resources | Educational content shared with families. | Waitlist → Active |
| Branded Agency Page | Shareable landing page with full agency info and intake links. Acts as referral sheet, pseudo-website, and intake funnel. | Entry → Inquiry |
| Analytics | Track listing views, inquiry conversion, pipeline health. | Measurement |

### 4.3 The Funnel Visualization

From the agency's perspective, the client lifecycle is a funnel:

> **Discovery** (FindABATherapy listing, website, referrals, word of mouth)
> → **Capture** (Contact form, Intake form → enters Inquiry Inbox)
> → **Nurture** (Communication templates, insurance verification, document collection)
> → **Convert** (Assessment authorization, assessment scheduled & completed)
> → **Serve** (Active client, ongoing authorization management, reassessments)
> → **Retain or Transition** (Reauthorization, on hold, or discharge)

Every feature in the product accelerates movement through this funnel. The agency's branded intake forms capture families at the top. Communication templates keep them engaged through the middle. Client management tools ensure nothing falls through the cracks at the bottom.

## 6. What Needs to Change

The platform has strong functionality but is organized around product brands (Client Growth, Hiring, Operations) rather than the client lifecycle. The following changes reorganize and extend what exists to deliver the growth engine experience.

### 6.1 Dashboard Reorganization

The current three-workspace model (Client Growth / Hiring / Operations) fragments the client journey. The client CRM lives in Operations while the intake tools and inquiry inbox live in Client Growth. This split breaks the mental model of "one place for all things client."

**Current structure:**

- Client Growth: Overview, Notifications/Inbox, Profile, Locations, Analytics
- Hiring: Jobs, Applicants, Employees, Careers Page
- Operations: Overview, Clients, Tasks, Forms, Resources

**New structure:**

> **ALWAYS VISIBLE** (top of sidebar or topbar, with badges)
> - **Inbox** — Unread badge. Spans: client inquiries, job applications, system alerts.
> - **Tasks** — Count badge (overdue + due today). Spans: client tasks, employee credential tasks, generic tasks, auto-generated tasks.
>
> **CLIENTS** (Primary — where users live daily)
> - Pipeline — Visual overview of all clients by lifecycle stage with counts
> - All Clients — Full CRM list with search, filter, and profile access
> - Communications — Email templates, send from client profiles, send history
>
> **INTAKE & PAGES** (The branded tools that feed the client pipeline)
> - Intake Form — Setup and customize branded intake form
> - Contact Form — Setup and customize branded contact form
> - Resources — Parent resource center setup and content
> - Directory Listing — Your public FindABATherapy profile and listing status
>
> **TEAM** (Secondary — staff management and hiring)
> - Employees — Staff management with credential tracking
> - Jobs — Job posting management
> - Applicants — Application inbox and tracking
> - Careers Page — Branded careers page setup
>
> **COMPANY & SETTINGS**
> - Profile & Branding — Agency name, logo, description, contact info
> - Locations — Service location management
> - Analytics — Listing views, inquiry conversion, pipeline health
> - Billing — Subscription and payment management
> - Account — User settings, help, feedback

Tasks and Inbox are cross-cutting concepts that span clients, employees, and generic operational work. Burying Tasks inside a "Clients" section forces users to navigate INTO clients just to see their task list, which breaks the mental model of "what do I need to do today?" Similarly, the Inbox receives inquiries (client-related), job applications (team-related), and system notifications. Making them always-visible top-level items ensures they are accessible from anywhere.

**Badge data queries:**

- **Inbox badge:** Count of unread inquiry messages (existing query in inquiries.ts) + unread job applications (existing query in applications.ts).
- **Tasks badge:** Count of tasks where status != "Done" and due_date <= today (overdue) or due_date = today. Query from client_tasks table.

**Key principles of this reorganization:**

- **Clients is the default landing section.** When a user logs in, they see their client pipeline — not a marketing overview. This reinforces that the product is about managing and growing their caseload.
- **Tasks and Inbox are always visible.** They don't belong to any one section. They surface what needs attention across the entire product.
- **Intake tools are directly connected to clients.** The forms and directory listing that bring families IN are grouped with the clients they produce, making the flow from capture to management obvious.
- **Team is secondary but accessible.** Hiring features are not hidden, but they're not competing for attention with the client lifecycle. Agencies use them when they need to hire, which is driven by client growth.
- **Company settings are stable and rarely changed.** Profile, locations, and billing are set up once and updated occasionally. They don't need to be in the primary workflow.

### 6.2 Onboarding Flow Rework

The current onboarding asks users to choose their intent (Find ABA Therapy / Hire Staff / Both). This creates the same positioning ambiguity the product has externally. The new onboarding should be opinionated and guide every user through the client acquisition setup first.

**New onboarding flow:**

1. **Sign Up** — Email and password. No plan selection. (Unchanged)
2. **Company Profile** — Agency name, contact email, upload logo. 60 seconds. (Unchanged)
3. **Location** — Primary service address, service modes (in-home, center, telehealth, school), accepting new clients toggle. 90 seconds. (Unchanged)
4. **Details** — Headline, description, ages served, languages, diagnoses, specialties. All fields available to everyone (previously Pro-gated). These enrich the directory listing. 2–3 minutes.
5. **Branded Pages Preview (NEW)** — Auto-generated preview cards showing what the agency's branded pages look like using their logo, name, and location data. The star of this step is the **Branded Agency Page** — a full, shareable landing page that functions as a pseudo-website, referral sheet, and intake funnel all in one. It displays everything the agency just entered (name, logo, description, locations, services, ages, languages, specialties, insurance) in a professional layout with prominent "Contact Us" and "Submit Intake" CTAs. Additional cards shown: Branded Contact Form, Branded Intake Form, Branded Careers Page, Branded Parent Resources. Below the previews: "Upgrade to Pro to activate these pages, plus full client lifecycle management, communication templates, analytics, and hiring tools." Two buttons: "Upgrade to Pro" (goes to Stripe checkout) and "Continue with Free" (skips to next step).
6. **Go Live on Directory** — Preview and publish FindABATherapy listing. Listing includes all detail fields they just filled in. (Unchanged)
7. **Success** — Celebration screen. Free users see: "Your listing is live!" with next actions ("Share your listing," "Upgrade to activate intake forms"). Pro users see: "You're all set!" with next actions ("Share your intake form," "Add your first client," "Post your first job").

> **Key Design Principle: No Configuration Required for Preview**
> The branded page previews are auto-generated from the company profile data the user already entered (name, logo, location, contact email). There is no configuration step. The user sees a preview card for each branded page type, showing their logo and agency name in a professional layout. This makes the upgrade moment feel effortless — "this already exists, just activate it."

**Why this onboarding converts:**

- Users have already invested time entering their info (sunk cost, endowment effect).
- They can see exactly what they'd get — not a feature list, but a visual preview.
- The upgrade decision is specific and contextual, not abstract.

**First-session activation targets:** After completing onboarding, the user should have a company profile with their name and logo, at least one service location, all directory detail fields filled in, a live listing on FindABATherapy, and a clear understanding that the next step is adding clients and using the lifecycle tools. If they can accomplish this in under 5 minutes, activation is successful.

## 7. What Needs to Be Built

The following features are new additions required to complete the client lifecycle engine. They are listed in order of strategic priority.

### 7.1 Communication Templates System (High Priority)

This is the single most important new feature. The ability to send branded, lifecycle-stage emails to families from within a client's profile transforms the platform from a passive CRM into an active engagement tool.

**How It Works:**

1. **Template library:** A set of pre-built email templates mapped to client lifecycle stages. Each template has a subject line, body content with merge fields (client name, parent name, agency name, etc.), and consistent company branding (logo, colors, footer).
2. **Send from client profile:** On any client's profile page, the user clicks "Send Communication" and selects a template. The template auto-fills with data from the client's record.
3. **Preview and edit:** Before sending, the user sees a full preview of the email with all merge fields populated. They can edit the subject line, body text, and any template fields. The template provides structure but the user has full control.
4. **Send via Resend:** The email is sent directly from the app using the existing Resend integration. The "from" address uses the agency's branded email or a Behavior Work sending address. The email is HTML-formatted with the agency's logo and consistent styling.
5. **Log on client profile:** Every sent communication is logged on the client's profile with timestamp, template used, and content. This creates a communication history visible to anyone on the team.

**Email Templates:**

Each template includes the following merge fields, auto-populated from the client's profile: `{client_name}`, `{parent_name}`, `{parent_email}`, `{agency_name}`, `{agency_phone}`, `{agency_website}`, `{agency_logo}`, `{insurance_name}`, `{assessment_date}`, `{assessment_time}`, `{assessment_location}`, `{team_members}`, `{start_date}`, `{resources_link}`, `{intake_link}`.

**Implementation Approach:**

Send directly from the app via Resend rather than pre-filling a mailto: link. Reasons: Resend is already integrated and proven in the codebase. Direct sending supports full HTML formatting with agency branding. Mailto: links have character limits, don't support HTML, and behave inconsistently across browsers and email clients. Direct sending enables communication logging on the client profile. Future: delivery tracking, open rates, and automated sending can build on this foundation.

The email preview/edit step before sending ensures agencies maintain control while benefiting from the structure and branding of templates.

### 7.2 Automated Task Creation Pattern (High Priority)

Introduce a new system pattern where tasks are automatically created based on dates or criteria. This eliminates manual follow-up tracking and ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

**Pattern Design:**

The automated task system works by defining triggers that create tasks when conditions are met:

- **Date-based triggers:** When a date field approaches or passes (e.g., credential expiration, authorization end date, reassessment due date), a task is automatically created a configurable number of days before.
- **Status-based triggers:** When a client's status changes (e.g., moved to Waitlist), related follow-up tasks can be auto-generated.
- **Recurring triggers:** For ongoing needs like monthly check-ins on waitlisted families.

**First Implementation: Credential Expiration Tasks**

The first use of this pattern is staff credential tracking. When an employee's credential is approaching expiration, a task is automatically created for review: Employee profile gets a new "Credentials" section. Each credential has: Credential Name, Expiration Date, Notes. 30 days before expiration: system creates a task titled "Review expiring credential: [Name] for [Employee]" with the expiration date as the due date. Task appears in the centralized Tasks view and can be assigned and tracked.

**Second Implementation: Authorization Expiration Tasks**

Client service authorizations have end dates. When an authorization is approaching expiration: 30 days before authorization end date: auto-create task "Submit reauthorization for [Client Name]". Links to the client profile for easy access to authorization details. This prevents lapses in service that lose revenue and disrupt family care.

**Technical Approach:**

Implement as a scheduled check (daily cron or on-login query) that scans for upcoming dates and creates tasks that don't already exist. This avoids duplicate tasks and works regardless of when the user logs in. The pattern should be generalized so new trigger types can be added with minimal code.

### 7.3 Staff Credential Tracking (Medium Priority)

Add a lightweight credential tracking section to employee profiles. This keeps the scope simple while enabling the automated task pattern above.

**Feature Scope:**

- **Add credential:** Name (e.g., "RBT Certification", "BCBA License", "Background Check"), Expiration Date, Notes.
- **View credentials:** List on employee profile showing credential name, expiration date, status indicator (current/expiring soon/expired), and notes.
- **Automated tasks:** Triggers the automated task creation pattern 30 days before expiration.

This is intentionally simple — three fields per credential plus automation. It solves the real problem (things expire and nobody notices) without overbuilding a credential management system.

### 7.4 Referral Source Tracking (Medium Priority)

Add a "Referral Source" field to client profiles and intake/contact forms so agencies can track where families are coming from.

**Implementation:**

- **Client profile field:** Dropdown with options: FindABATherapy Directory, Agency Website, Pediatrician Referral, School Referral, Insurance Provider, Word of Mouth, Social Media, Google Search, Other (with text field).
- **Intake form field:** Optional "How did you hear about us?" question on intake and contact forms. If submitted from FindABATherapy directory, auto-populate as "FindABATherapy Directory."
- **Reporting:** Simple breakdown of referral sources on the analytics page. Over time, this data becomes valuable for understanding which channels drive the most clients.

### 7.5 Client Pipeline Dashboard (Medium Priority)

The "Pipeline" page under Clients should provide a visual overview of all clients organized by lifecycle stage. This is the first thing the user sees when they open the dashboard.

**Design:**

- **Summary cards:** One card per lifecycle stage showing the count of clients in that stage. Click a card to filter the client list to that stage.
- **Quick actions:** From the pipeline view, the user can quickly move clients between stages, send a communication, or view the client profile.
- **Alerts:** Surface important items: overdue tasks, expiring authorizations, clients waiting more than X days without communication.

This replaces the current "Client Growth Overview" which shows listing metrics. The pipeline view centers the user's attention on their actual clients and what needs attention.

### 7.6 Branded Agency Page (High Priority)

Most small ABA agencies either don't have a website, or they have one that's outdated, incomplete, and disconnected from their intake workflow. The Branded Agency Page solves this by auto-generating a professional, shareable landing page from the data the agency has already entered into the platform. Zero extra work — it just exists.

**What It Is:**

A standalone, branded page at a clean URL (e.g., `behaviorwork.com/p/agency-slug`) that displays the agency's complete profile in a polished, professional layout. It pulls from everything already in the system: agency name, logo, headline, description, all locations with addresses and service modes, ages served, languages spoken, diagnoses supported, clinical specialties, insurance accepted, photos, and video embed. At the bottom, prominent CTAs link directly to the branded contact form and/or intake form.

It is essentially a private, branded version of the agency's FindABATherapy directory listing — but designed to stand on its own as a shareable page rather than living inside the directory UI.

**Use Cases:**

- **Referral sheet for providers.** An agency sends this link to a pediatrician's office, a speech therapy company, a school district, or an insurance care coordinator. Instead of a PDF or business card, the referral source has a live, always-current page with all the agency's details and a direct intake link.
- **Pseudo-website for agencies without one.** Many small agencies run on word of mouth and don't have a real web presence. This page gives them a professional, mobile-friendly landing page they can link from their Google Business Profile, social media, or email signature.
- **Parent information page.** When a parent calls asking "what do you do?" the agency can text or email this link. It answers every question a parent would have — services, locations, ages, insurance — and funnels them directly into the intake form.
- **Cross-agency referrals.** When one ABA agency is full, they can share another agency's branded page rather than just a phone number. This builds the inter-agency referral network organically.

**Why It's High Priority:**

- Requires zero new data entry — uses existing profile data.
- Directly drives client acquisition by turning every referral into a live, interactive intake funnel.
- Massively strengthens the "put your info in, get a website out" value proposition.
- Strengthens the onboarding "wow" moment — it's the star of the branded preview step.
- Creates a natural upgrade trigger: free users can see the page but the intake/contact forms it links to require Pro.

**What's on the Page:**

- Agency logo and name (prominent header)
- Headline and description
- All locations with full addresses, service modes (in-home, center, telehealth, school), and accepting-new-clients status
- Ages served, languages spoken, diagnoses supported, clinical specialties
- Insurance carriers accepted
- Photo gallery and video embed (if available)
- "Contact Us" button → links to branded contact form (Pro)
- "Start Intake" button → links to branded intake form (Pro)
- Link to parent resource center (if configured)
- Agency contact info (phone, email, website) in the footer

**Relationship to FindABATherapy Listing:**

The FindABATherapy listing is public and discoverable through directory search — it's designed for families browsing the directory. The Branded Agency Page is private (not indexed, not in the directory) and designed to be intentionally shared. Same data, different context. The listing catches families who are searching. The agency page catches families who are referred.

**Technical Approach:**

This is a server-rendered page at `/p/[slug]` that queries the agency's profile, locations, and service data. No new data models needed — it reads from the existing company profile, locations, services, and media tables. The page is publicly accessible via the URL but not indexed by search engines (noindex meta tag) and not linked from the directory. Styling uses the agency's brand color (already stored) for accent elements.

**Gating:**

- Free users: The page exists and is viewable, but the "Contact Us" and "Start Intake" CTAs show a message like "Contact [agency name] at [phone/email]" with basic contact info instead of linking to the branded forms. This still provides referral value while creating upgrade pressure.
- Pro users: Full page with live links to branded contact and intake forms. The page becomes a true intake funnel.

### 8.2 Pricing Philosophy

- **Free gets you discovered.** A full, detailed listing on FindABATherapy with all profile fields, up to 3 locations, up to 3 photos, basic client tracking (10 records), and 1 job posting. Generous enough to be genuinely useful.
- **Pro gives you the growth engine.** Full client lifecycle tools, branded intake, communications, task automation, and hiring. This is where the overwhelming majority of agencies should land. The value is immediately clear: one new client from the platform pays for years of subscription.
- **Enterprise gives you scale.** Unlimited everything for agencies with multiple locations and large caseloads. The per-client economics still work at this price point.
- **Featured placement is the only add-on.** Keep pricing simple. The only upsell beyond the plan tier is featured state placement on FindABATherapy for agencies that want enhanced visibility. This is a distribution boost, not a core feature.

### 8.3 Conversion Strategy

The free plan creates natural pressure to upgrade through workflow limits, not data restrictions. The key upgrade triggers:

- **Client record limit (10).** Once an agency has 10 clients tracked, they need Pro. This happens fast for any active agency.
- **Branded agency page with intake links.** Free users get the agency page as an informational/referral tool, but the intake and contact form CTAs only work on Pro. They can share the page and see its value — they just can't close the loop without upgrading.
- **Communication templates.** Free users can see the template library but can't send. This shows the value without giving it away.
- **Analytics.** Free users don't see listing performance data. Upgrading to Pro reveals how many families are viewing their listing, creating urgency to convert those views.

The upgrade trigger shifts from "I can't fill in my profile" to "I need better tools to manage the clients coming in." This is a healthier, more sustainable conversion motion.
